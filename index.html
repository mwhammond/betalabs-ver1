Advisors increase start-up sucess


At Imperial Innovations we regularly meet amazing teams that lack the right connections to fully understand the drivers, structure and incentives of their desired market vertical. We also meet a large number of senior industry professionals who are keen to work with start-ups to add value and enable disruption within their industries. Unfortunately finding each other can take months and many wasted evenings of random networking events, so we thought that there must be a better way (plus we can’t keep all the potential connections in our heads!).

We built this service to bring the best start-ups and advisors together more efficiently to build even better ventures.

Recent matches

High quality early stage ventures which we have recently connected to sector expertise expertise.

Aqdot

Case study: FMCG

Aqdot is a Cambridge based spin out that has developed novel fabrication technology for smart capsules that possess customisable properties to suit the needs of a variety of industries. Neil MacGilp who had spent 30 years at Procter & Gamble worked with the team to position the technology for adoption in the FMCG industry. On the back of this Aqdot were able to successfully close a £1m funding round.

Gyro Glove

Case study: Engineering & healthcare

Gyroglove stabilises Parkinson's tremors allowing patients to live a more normal life. This early stage accelerator based team brings together medical and engineering expertise but required an understanding of manufacturing at scale and selling in to the NHS. Ian Church was able to provide this expertise with 20 years in hitech engineering as well as 8 years leading healthcare firm CSH Surrey.

Econic

Case study: Polymers

Econic is developing a new catalyst technology that enables CO2 to replace petrochemical feedstock in plastic precursors. Advisor David Morgan began working with the technologist founders to provide catalyst industry expertise and financial strategy including the opportunity to outsource the catalyst manufacture. The company has raised over $12m in venture funding since his involvement.
